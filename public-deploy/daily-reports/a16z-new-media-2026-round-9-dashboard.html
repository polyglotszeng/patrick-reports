📺 The Media Game Has Changed

a16z New Media Summit — Marc Andreessen × Ben Horowitz × Erik Torenberg (host) × Gaby Goldberg

核心论点 (Core Thesis): 旧媒体（防御、品牌抽象、说教风）已死。 新媒体 = 进攻、创始人即品牌、3 小时长播客、真实人格 + 高对抗性。 "Old media is defense oriented. New media is offense." — Marc @ [39]
  1. Legacy press 已变成 adversarial — 2017 后从"客观报道"翻转为"权力压制真相"（"power to truth"），创始人无法通过传统媒体拿到想要的故事。
  2. Founder-as-Brand / Going Direct — Elon = SpaceX、Alex Karp = Palantir、Palmer = Anduril。公司品牌 = 人；创始人必须亲自上场讲 3 小时故事。
  3. 真实性 > 媒体训练 — 像跟朋友吃午饭一样说话；"if you know it intimately, you should be able to talk about it viscerally."
  4. Polarizing beats being liked — 想被所有人喜欢 = 无聊 = 营销自杀。要让对的人恨你，触发对的人爱你。
  5. Bigger story (Karp Playbook) — 别讲"我 + 公司 + 产品"；讲世界上最有趣的事，然后把公司 plug 进去。

⚡ 核心论点与五大要点

本场 a16z New Media Summit 圆桌由 Erik Torenberg 主持，Marc、Ben、Gaby 三人轮流出场，给出了对当下媒体生态（2026 H1）的实战框架。下面是从 41 分钟对话中提炼的 5 个高密度论点：

BIG SHIFT@ [39]

1️⃣ 旧媒体防御、新媒体进攻

"Old media is defense oriented. New media is offense." — Marc 在开场抛出这一框架，并把旧媒体的 "minimum controversy"（不出错即胜利）评为新媒体的死亡陷阱。

GO DIRECT@ [00:11:58]

2️⃣ 创始人 = 品牌

"Is it Palantir or is it Alex? Is it Anduril or is it Palmer?" — Marc 给出名单：Elon / Karp / Palmer / Jensen。Elon 被 Democrats 骂，不是因为 SpaceX，而是因为 Elon 个人即品牌。

AUTHENTICITY@ [56]

3️⃣ 真实性 > 媒体训练

"Say all the things in public that you would say if you were sitting having lunch with a friend." — Marc 转述当年 60 Minutes 制片人 Lee Les Elden 的训练哲学。

POLARIZE@ [00:23:42]

4️⃣ 让人恨比让人爱重要

"Nobody writes a puff piece on Rupert Murdoch or Elon Musk. When you get to a certain size, people hate you. And that's good, because that means you did it." — Marc @ [00:24:13]

KARP MOVE@ [00:33:47]

5️⃣ 讲更大的故事 (Karp Playbook)

Alex Karp 从不正面谈 Palantir，只谈 US military / superintelligence / neurodivergence — Palantir 隐式插入。"Find the most interesting story that you can plug your company into."

📌 当下媒体的"新规则"清单 (Marc 总结 @ [00]-[30])

  • ❌ 不要 be interested — 在旧媒体里"无聊"是金科玉律，在新媒体里无聊 = 死亡。
  • ✅ 不要 inside-out — 别从"我和我的公司"出发；先想"世界上最有趣的事是什么"，再 plug 自己。
  • ✅ Unlimited formats / unlimited channels — 旧媒体的"窄吸管"已倒塌，没有 channel 的瓶颈。
  • ✅ Brand = person — 1930s 之前的 Ford Motor / Edison Electric 才是常态；抽象 corporate brand 只是中央化媒体的副作用。
  • ✅ Long form = new medium — Charlie Rose 午夜 1 小时 → Joe Rogan 3 小时 → 长 Substack。Re-rise of "interesting people having interesting conversations."

📰 媒体环境变化 — Legacy Press 已变成 Adversarial

本节主题从 [00:06:59] 进入。Marc 的核心论点：传统媒体从 "impartial journalism + speak truth to power" 翻转为 "power to truth" — 也就是说媒体变成权力本身，用来 intimidate 不愿配合的创始人。

🕰️ Marc 的媒体史时间线

[00:06:59]
传统媒体的两大自我定义功能: (1) impartial / objective journalism ("voice from nowhere"), (2) speak truth to power ("afflict the comfortable, comfort the afflicted")。
— Marc Andreessen
[00:08:08]
"But it devolved into power to truth." — 第二个功能 swamped 第一个；press 变成 intimidation tool。 Ben 补刀: "yeah, the line between objective and activist."
— Marc / Ben
[00:08:53]

"2017 things changed" — agenda-driven 模式 takeover，Marc 认为永远 unwound 不了。给创始人的 practical reality: "There is no way to get to a story that you're going to like through traditional media."
— Marc
[00:10:02]
新方法 = 两条腿走路: (a) Go Direct (自己频道 + ally 频道), (b) 新 voices / 新 media。 Legacy press "bananas" 创造 massive opportunity。

[00:10:23]

✅ 真实性 vs 媒体训练 — Why Authenticity Beats Training

本节从 [56] 进入。Erik 提的第一个硬问题："为什么 authenticity 那么重要? 为什么太多 buttoned-up / media-trained 是 founder 常见错误?" Marc 用自己 90 年代的 media training horror story 作为反证。

😱 Marc 的 Media Training Horror Story

HORROR@ [00:02:41]

"The meanest thing you can do"

90 年代 media training 标准流程: 把 CEO 放在镜头前 60 分钟 → 强制看完整录像回放 (no fast forward) → 教练当场 critique → 可能再看一遍。

"You discover how much you hate your voice, how much you hate how you look. If your ego just survives the beating, you go on to a successful career."

OUTCOME@ [00:03:26]

"Important people seemed plastic"

结果: trained CEOs 上 TV → "most innocuous things possible" → 评分标准 = "minimum controversy" = 没人 news = "I didn't make any news, I'm proud."

Ben 补充: "All news is bad news." (指 trained CEO 的世界观)

UNORTHODOX COACH@ [00:04:30]

Lee Les Elden 的反训练

a16z 当时雇了 "best media trainer" = Lowell Bergman 的前制片人 (60 Minutes 团队)。 他开场说: "We're not doing classic media training."

训练只有一件事: "say all the things in public that you would say if you were sitting having lunch with a friend."

COACH'S RULE@ [00:05:08]

"If you know it intimately…"

他原话: "If you are on stage or in an interview and you don't know that topic inside out already, like what the hell are you doing there? If you know it intimately, you should be able to talk about it in a viscerally interesting way."

所有 "pivot / Jedi mind trick" 的训练技巧都只是辅助 — 主轴是真实性。

🎯 Ben 的 "Jedi Pivot" 解释

[00:05:42]Ben Horowitz
"It's the Jedi knight thing that made that whole approach work. One of the ways you talk naturally is you just refuse to answer the bad questions and you just substitute in your good question."
→ Pivot 不是 manipulation — 是 "I have my own agenda and I'll route around your question." 这与 authenticity 不矛盾，因为 pivot = "I know what I want to say."

⚔️ Polarizing Beats Being Liked

本节从 [00:23:42] 进入。Marc 给出可能是全场最反直觉的 hard truth: "You really want people to hate you and you want people to love you, but you don't want to be neutral."

💀 死亡区 = Lukewarm

CORE TRUTH@ [00:23:42]

"You want people to hate you and love you"

"You can't be interesting and not have people both hate you and love you. There's too much jealousy and hatred in the world to not have it be that. As soon as you get big, that's what happens."

REFERENCE@ [00:24:13]

"Nobody writes a puff piece on Murdoch or Musk"

Ben's framing: "Have all the right enemies. Picking the right people to hate you that galvanizes the people who love you."

"If you take it like 'oh, I got to stop doing that so people like me,' you'll ruin your marketing."

PRACTICAL@ [00:22:14]

Degenerate case = Twitter replies

Ben: "If you start answering the X replies of people who have 50 followers — my father always did that — there's just like a colossal waste of time and you're amplifying somebody who has no audience."

→ Polarize ≠ 攻击 random accounts. Polarize = 跟有 audience 的人 battle.

🎯 Picking Battles — When to Fight Back

本节从 [00:20:20] 进入。Erik 直接问 Marc/Ben: "How do you decide when to fight back versus stay quiet?" — 这是 founder 实操里最难的问题之一。

🎯 The Two Buckets Framework

❌ DO NOT FIGHT — "Degenerate case"

  • Anon Twitter accounts with 50 followers
  • People with no audience amplifying nothing
  • "They didn't do anything in life to have the right to talk to you"
  • Cost = colossal waste of time + amplify their reach

✅ FIGHT BACK — "Real opportunity"

  • Someone with actual audience attacks you
  • Legitimate critique in mainstream press
  • Issue where you have unique point of view
  • Cost = free distribution for your POV

🔥 Marc 的 Instagram/NYT 案例 — "Best post I ever wrote"

[00:22:14]Marc Andreessen
"We got into this kerfuffle over Instagram with The New York Times. I responded — it was the biggest post I ever wrote. Everybody was like, 'Yeah f*** The New York Times.' It took us from here to here in one shot. Just cuz everybody loves a fight."
→ "Fights are good for brand building. But you just got to pick the right fight to not just build somebody else's brand." — 这条比 "always fight" 更精细: 必须 pick the right fight。

🤝 Ben 的 a16z 内部应对方式

[00:22:58]Ben Horowitz
"When I first joined, I would bring up these negative tweets and say 'Hey, these Anons are saying bad things about us.' And you guys would be like, 'Who cares?' In fact, part of our success means we're going to have more and more people saying negative things."
→ 早期 Ben 学到的 lesson: 内部 escalation 本身是 noise. 真正的过滤 = "is this worth my founder attention right now?"

📌 The Hater Reframe (Ben @ [00:23:05])

[00:23:05]Ben Horowitz
"Even if you think we're wrong about everything, what is better in this world than the transfer of wealth from people who have a lot of money to people who have ideas and want to build something? Like that's still a good for humanity. So shut up to you, hater."
→ 把 critic 的逻辑翻转: 即使他们指控 100% 成立, 公司行为仍是 net positive → critic 在反对 net-positive. 这是 founder-grade 的 pick-battle 防御话术.

👥 团队建设 — Hiring for New Media

本节从 [00:25:06] 进入。Erik 问: "新 media 时代, 怎么 hire 对的人?" Ben + Gaby + Marc 给出三段递进答案.

1️⃣ Ben 的第一原则 — 旧媒体训练是负债

[00:25:06]Ben Horowitz
"If you're trained in old media, it's very very very hard to do new media. You have to be a very exceptional person to make that transition. If you spent 10 years doing old media, there are laws of physics, rules of the game, things you do every single time in terms of vetting reporters to rude Q&As — everything about it is opposite world."
→ Old media = defense muscle memory. New media = offense muscle memory. 两者 transfer rate 极低. → Hire signal: already built audience / brand on something.

2️⃣ Erik 自己就是 a16z 的 Hire Signal 范本

[00:26:21]Marc Andreessen
"What we looked for when we brought you on, Eric: the best thing on new media is — have you done it? Have you built some brand on something and some audience? Do you know how to build an audience? Because that's the core thing. If you can't do that, it doesn't matter if you've got this skill and that skill."
→ a16z hire Erik = 因为他 "done it" (Theoretical Ventures / Twitter presence / 新媒体 fluency). 这是 explicit 招聘标准.

3️⃣ Marc 加码 — "Proof of work even though they weren't doing the thing"

[00:26:53]Marc Andreessen
"Alex Danco, Henry, Brent — they're product managers or founders or investors, but they were obsessed with the discourse. They were listening to podcasts, they were writing themselves. You could tell in the proof of work even though they weren't doing the thing."
→ Hire signal tier 2: 即使没做过, 但 "obsessed with the discourse / writing themselves" = 自我证明已经在练习场。 没有这条 hire = 训练成本太高.

4️⃣ Storytelling 作为稀缺技能

[00:27:11]Marc Andreessen
"The reason why Alex [Danco] is so good is because he's a world-class storyteller. Forget just take marketing out of it for a second. Can you put together a story that somebody wants to read or listen to? That's a real skill. The elite level of that is way higher than the average level of that."
→ Storytelling ≠ 旧 marketing. Storytelling = "puts together a story with tension, beginning, end." 这是 a16z 团队的核心筛选标准 — 旧 marketing hire 大概率失败.

✅ The "Authentic Storytelling" Disconnect

Ben @ [00:27:40] 提出一个重要的 inter-section: 传统 reporter 会把 "going direct" 框定为 "propaganda / marketing"。a16z 反驳:

[00:28:20]Ben Horowitz
"Storytelling of like here's what's actually happening — in an interesting way, with tension, with a beginning and an end, that somebody is interested in following the whole way. That's not propaganda. That's just good communication."
→ Reframe: Going direct = "actually explain yourself + the world." 这个 reframe 对 hire 评估很关键 — 候选人不应认为 "storytelling" = manipulation.

❌ Founder Messaging Mistakes — Gaby 的两大错误

本节从 [00:30:03] 进入。Gaby Goldberg 给两个高度实战化的 founder error。

Mistake #1 — Distribution over Message

[00:30:03]Gaby Goldberg
"It's a trap to fall into because the timeline feels so addicting. A lot of founders really over index on distribution and tactics before actually getting the message right. Distribution is really just a multiplier on the message. And so if the message is wrong, now you've amplified something that is either irrelevant for your business or not the thing that your audience needs to hear — or maybe worst of all, it's uninteresting."
→ The math: wrong message × 100x distribution = "told everybody that you care about that you're not very interesting." — distribution 不是杠杆, distribution 是 multiplier on already-correct message.

⚡ Mistake #2 — Inputs vs Outputs

[00:31:30]Gaby Goldberg
"You can get the message wrong if you focus too much on the inputs as opposed to the outputs that you want to drive towards. Just because everything is true doesn't mean it's all relevant or strategic for your business. Start with the outcome. Do we want to sell to a certain type of enterprise customer? Do we want to hire a certain type of engineer who believes a certain thing about us? So now you can kind of work backwards from there."
→ Inverse-PR: 不要 "say everything you could say", 而是 "reverse from outcome → figure out exactly what to say." Counter-intuitive: 越成功的公司越容易踩 — 因为太多东西可讲.

📌 Marc 补刀: "Marketing an empty box"

[00:32:50]Marc Andreessen
"Really determines how you have to put the team together because if you put together a team that you can't have a conversation with where you're listening on your message — then you've kind of done yourself a disservice. You need the team to help make the story great before they go. It's really easy to go 'Okay, market this empty box.' There are a few marketers who could probably do that, but it's hard."
→ If team doesn't have opinion on message → forced to ship empty-box marketing. Team 必须 invested in message — not just distribution.

→ Patrick 应该 ask: "Without Elon's audience, can a B2B SaaS founder at $5M ARR apply Karp Playbook? Probably not — but Ben's 'right enemies' or Gaby's 'output-first' might work."

BLIND SPOT 2Political polarization

⚔️ "Polarize" 框架的危险面

Marc @ [00:23:42]: "want people to hate you" — 这听起来 badass, 但在没有 Marc / Karp 这种 "default credibility" 的 founder 手里, 极化 = 引来真的 threats (doxxing / harassment / regulatory attention)。 a16z portfolio companies 受 SEC 监管 + 媒体覆盖, 极化的 downside 对他们有 firewall。 对 early-stage founder 没有。

→ Patrick 的 application: 在 apply "polarize" 之前先 check — "do I have political, legal, financial, personal firewall to absorb the haters?" If not, default to "be less boring, but don't seek enemies."

BLIND SPOT 3Media history overclaim

📚 "2017 changed everything" 简化

Marc @ [00:09:28]: "2017 things changed, and I think probably will never unwind." — 这把 2016 → 2017 当作 atomic 转折点, 但 press trust decline 实际是 20+ 年 gradual curve (Pew 数据: 2000 信任度 50% → 2016 18% → 2024 32% 部分反弹)。 Marc 把 gradual 描述为 sudden = rhetorical but inaccurate。

→ Marc's argument is correct in direction (press 失去 founder-friendly), wrong in chronology. Use as directional thesis, not historical fact.

BLIND SPOT 4Authenticity paradox

🪞 "Be authentic" 是 advice, 不是 strategy

Marc 的核心 message = "just be authentic / viscerally interesting." — 但 "authentic" 是 trait, 不是 lever。 Karp 的 authenticity 是 because he genuinely doesn't care about Palantir — 这是 personality, not technique。 对 founder who ISN'T naturally charismatic + world-class storyteller, "be authentic" = no actionable advice.

→ More actionable version: "Be authentic about ONE thing you're genuinely obsessed with, AND have a team to amplify that obsession into 3-hour long-form." Still hard, but at least decomposable.

BLIND SPOT 5Going direct ≠ democratic

🏛️ Going Direct 假设 founder 视角正确

Marc's "go direct" framework 假设 founder's POV 是值得 amplify 的。 但: (a) 多数 founder 对自己产品有 bias (sunk cost / survivorship), (b) 监管 / 安全 / 社会外部性 不在 founder 视角里。 当 founder 跑 "going direct" 绕过记者 = also 绕过 accountability checks。 这对 crypto / biotech / defense (Palantir / Anduril / SpaceX) 尤其危险。

→ Going Direct = powerful, 但需要配套: independent board, safety review, ethics committee. Patrick 应 not just optimize for founder's reach — optimize for founder's reach + accountability.

LIMITNot addressed

❓ "What if your product is boring but real?"

Marc 反复强调 "rule of new media is it has to be interesting" — 但 boring-but-real 产品 (insurance, compliance, accounting infra) 是经济 backbone. 这些 founder 无法 "plug into a bigger interesting story" 因为 they ARE the boring story。 整个 framework 假设 founder can choose to attach to bigger narrative — 对 boring-but-essential 产品不可行。

→ 缺失的另一半: "Boring-but-essential" 产品的 distribution 框架。 也许是旧 media (industry trade journals) + niche conferences + LinkedIn (Marc 不会 cover) — but worth flagging.
