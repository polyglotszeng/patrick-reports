This is the ninth edition of the AI Index. Frontier models are converging at the technical frontier — Anthropic, xAI, Google and OpenAI sit within 25 Elo points on the Arena Leaderboard. The U.S.–China performance gap has effectively closed (2.7% as of March 2026), but capability is still scaling faster than the institutional systems meant to manage it. In 2025 generative AI reached 53% population adoption within three years, global corporate AI investment more than doubled to $581.7B, and the average training compute continued to grow 3.3× per year. Yet documented AI incidents climbed 55% to 362, and responsible-AI reporting still lags capability reporting. The data does not point in one direction — it reveals a field scaling faster than the systems around it can adapt.

#2

Training Cost Trends

Training cost has risen roughly 3.3× per year since 2022. The most compute-intensive models in the Epoch AI dataset — Grok 3, Llama 4 Behemoth — required upward of 100 MW during training. Carbon emissions from training rose from ~0.01 tons CO2e (AlexNet, 2012) to ~72,816 tons CO2e (Grok 4, 2025).

Unit: Estimated training cost (USD millions, log scale) Source: HAI 2026 AI Index, Chapter 1.4 Energy & Environmental Impact; Epoch AI model-level data
Key takeaways (3)
  • Training compute has grown 3.3× per year since 2022 (chapter highlight #1)
  • Grok 4 emissions: 72,816 tons CO2e — more than a typical car over its lifetime (63 tons)
  • DeepSeek v3 emitted only ~597 tons CO2e despite comparable size, showing hardware & grid matter

Global Private AI Investment by Region

In 2025, U.S. private AI investment reached $285.9B — 23.1× China's $12.4B and 48.5× the U.K.'s $5.9B. Generative AI accounted for $170.9B (nearly half). Newly funded AI companies grew 70.8% YoY, but capital concentrated at the top: 28 events over $1B, up from 15.

AI Compute Energy Efficiency

Leading ML hardware delivers ~10× more FLOP/s per watt than a decade ago. Nvidia B200 and Google TPU v5e rank among the most efficient. However, models have scaled faster than efficiency gains, so total training power draw continues to climb — Grok 3 and Llama 4 Behemoth exceeded 100 MW during training.

Unit: FLOP/s per watt (log scale, leading hardware) Source: HAI 2026 AI Index, Chapter 1.4.1 Training; Epoch AI

  • GPT-4o annual inference water use > drinking-water needs of 1.2M people
  • Training emissions: 0.01 tons (AlexNet 2012) → 72,816 tons (Grok 4 2025)

AI Productivity Gains by Sector

Productivity gains concentrate in structured, measurable work. Studies report +14% in customer support, +26% in software development, +50% in marketing output. Gains are smaller — sometimes negative — on tasks requiring judgment. AI agent deployment remains in single digits across almost all business functions.

Unit: Reported productivity gain % vs. control Source: HAI 2026 AI Index, Chapter 4 Economy; multiple controlled studies

  • Customer support studies: +14% to +15% gain
  • Software dev: +26% productivity
  • Marketing output: +50% (highest in published controlled studies)
  • Median AI agent deployment < 10% across business functions

Postsecondary AI-Related Degrees

Between 2024 and 2025, CS enrollment at U.S. four-year universities fell 11%. AI-related master's degrees grew 17% in 2023–24, and AI-software master's graduates rose 82% from 2022 to 2024. New AI PhDs in the U.S. and Canada rose 22% (2022→2024), and all of that growth went into academia, reversing a decade-long trend.
