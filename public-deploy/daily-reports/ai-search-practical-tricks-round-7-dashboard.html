🏥 Brain ultrasound — Olive's microbubble approach

The middle ground between EEG and electrodes

[0:35:59] EEG outside the head = safe but blurry. Electrodes in the brain = precise but invasive. Olive sits in between: no drilling, but ~100× the resolution of similar CT scans.

Mechanism: ultrasound waves scatter off moving blood cells → build a 3D map of blood flow & volume in the brain. Captures vascular activity that lights up when neurons fire.

How the resolution works

Uses microbubbles — tiny contrast bubbles injected into the bloodstream that reflect ultrasound strongly. Millions of moving bubbles stacked into one super-detailed 3D map. This week: most detailed vascular image of a living human brain captured via ultrasound without drilling the skull.

⚠️ Not actually non-invasive — the bubbles still have to enter the bloodstream; the team has not addressed that risk in the blog.

Why this matters + where you can run it

End goal: insights into Alzheimer's, traumatic brain injury, brain function in real time. Full pipeline open-sourced (data + code) — but you still need lab-grade ultrasound equipment, so "do this at home" is not feasible.

⚠️ Limits, caveats & industry skepticism

Credibility is medium (industry news aggregation channel — not non-causal, but also not first-party research). Treat the following claims with normal engineering skepticism.

📛 Named critics / advocate positions

📛 "Big-closed is broken" — Yann LeCun camp

Position: closed frontier labs (OpenAI, Anthropic) gate access by regulatory capture, the open-source wave (Ornith, Crea 2, DomainShuttle, Unzero, Arbor) proves the closed-lab tier is no longer necessary. [0:34:32]

Counter-evidence: GPT 5.6 Soul Ultra = new SOTA on Terminal Bench even vs Mythos (closed benefits still real).

📛 "Orchestrator = base model?" — Andrew Ng camp

Position: Sakana Fugu's "surpasses Mythos on knowledge" claim is misleading; ensemble results vs base-model benchmarks is apples-to-oranges. [0:40:21]

Counter-evidence: Routing IS a product, and users may prefer a "smart router" over a "smart single model". The comparison framing just needs to be honest.

📛 "Vendor charts always cherry-pick" — independent-eval camp

Position: ByteDance Seed 2.1's chart excludes Opus 4.8; Sakana Fugu's chart excludes per-component breakdown; GPT 5.6's chart excludes "exploit bench" likely on purpose. [0:22:57]

Counter-evidence: vendors are required to ship diff-friendly charts to investors. Cherry-picking is rational, not malicious — but the eval ecosystem is the immune system.

📛 "US gov is the new model gatekeeper" — sovereignty camp

Position: Mythos ban + 100-partner gate + GPT 5.6 preview constraint → "permanent underclass" for non-US / non-trusted-partner developers. Sovereignty implications per host. [0:33:26]

Counter-evidence: the gating is a defense posture (cyber / biosecurity); unrestrained Mythos-class models carry real catastrophic risk that the open-source wave doesn't (yet) make trivial.

📛 "Unzero / oscillator-based gen is overhyped" — diffusion-orthodox camp

Position: diffusion is the right inductive bias for image / video / audio; oscillator-based generation is a curiosity without industrial scale. [0:16:48]

Counter-evidence: Unzero is MIT-licensed, runs, and produces images. Even if quality is "proof-of-concept", the architectural option value is real.

📛 "AI-designed hardware is fine" — verification camp

Position: OpenAI × Broadcom "Jalapeno" built-in-9-months with OpenAI-models-helping-design is the FIRST credible "AI for silicon" case; gate at the verification step. [0:10:39]

Counter-evidence: hardware requires physical verification regardless of design agent; AGI-for-EDA is at most a 10-30% speedup, not a paradigm shift.

📌 Engineering caveats (per-story)

1. Seedance 2.5 — announced, not released

[0:20:13] ⚠️ Unveiled at an event; launch "expected in early July". Until you can actually prompt it and benchmark it, treat the 50-ref / 30-second / 4K claims as vendor claims, not measured results.

2. One Streamer — 192p proof of concept

[0:01:04] ⚠️ The 25fps / 200ms numbers are on a 192p, single-camera, single-model demo. No code yet — only the paper. Real consumer product could look very different.

3. OpenAI Jalapeno — no detailed report

[0:10:01] "Substantially better perf/watt than current SOTA accelerators" — but the detailed technical report is still coming. Don't price-in until you see the silicon.

4. Sakana Fugu — apples to oranges

[0:40:21] ❌ Misleading Claims "surpasses Claude Fable / Mythos" — but Fugu is an orchestrator, not a base model. Ensemble results naturally beat any single model. Closed-source, closed-allocation, expensive. Don't confuse "routing is good" with "Fugu is a new frontier LLM".

5. "Ban lifted" ≠ "you can use it"

[0:33:38] ⚠️ Mythos-ban was eased — but only for ~100 trusted partners & federal agencies. Same playbook for GPT 5.6 Soul preview. The "ban lifted" headline overstates the actual public access.

6. Seed 2.1 missing Opus 4.8

[0:22:57] Chart shows Opus 4.7, not 4.8. Whether intentional or accidental, it inflates Seed 2.1's relative position. Always pull the vendor chart and check N of comparison.

7. Olive brain ultrasound — invasive (still)

[0:35:59] ⚠️ "Non-invasive" framing is overstated: you still inject microbubbles into the bloodstream. The team has not addressed this risk in the blog.
