❌ 4 个虚假趋势（Joke Trends）

① Discomorphism

触发：Spotify 改 logo 为 disco ball-inspired shape（20 周年）

本质：只是 mirror-like texture = skeuomorphism 子集

"Discomorphism is not a design style in any way. It's just a mirror-like texture."

如果是真风格：需要 device camera 把你脸 fill 进镜子 → 算 augmented reality

② Metalmorphism

只是 metal material 的 sub-classification

"Metalmorphism is nothing new. It's just skeuomorphism because it's just a material."

③ Squircle / ④ Italicmorphism

Squircle = 特定 corner radius（friendly + processable）

Italicmorphism = 同理

都是 UX 细节，不是新趋势

Figma 恶搞: Balloonmorphism

"Looked a little bit AI generated. But balloons made with AI can pop."

💎 Glassmorphism 为何真正特别

"Glass is a material, obviously. But, in a way, this is a little bit more important than material itself, because all of the other materials are non-translucent. And just having that transparency, the way to see through something, allows you to have a different level of depth and context."

核心差异对比

MorphismMaterial关键特性
DiscomorphismMirrorOpaque
MetalmorphismMetalOpaque
NeumorphismSoft 3DOpaque
Glassmorphism Glass Translucent → context

💡 Glassmorphism 真正特别

  • 不只 "looks like glass"
  • 让你 see through → 看到下面的内容 = context
  • "Without that context, it would be just another material texture."
  • "None of the other morphisms do that. They're all opaque."
  • 模式无关：跟 flat design 和 skeuomorphism 都 work

🔘 Neumorphism（Minimalism 中间地带）

3 大风格

Minimalism

Flat, simple

Flat Design

2D, no depth

Skeuomorphism

Real material imitation

Neumorphism 定位

Minimalism + Flat + Skeuomorphism = odd middle ground

  • 极简
  • 没 texture
  • 没 many qualities
  • 只是 extruded from background

关于 Accessibility 争议（Malewicz 反驳）

"Accessibility issues are non-issues if you do the entire rest of the interface right. Because if everything has proper contrast, proper hierarchy, good typography, then just that little border around some things might not be visible to everyone, but they will still get the whole interface structure from everything else."

"This is just an extra, not a defining thing."

Squircle 插曲

Squircle = friendly + processable 的 sweet spot

  • 比 sharp corners 友好
  • 比 full circles 容易 processing
  • "Sharp corners = alert-prone, luxury, professional, not friendly"
  • "Circles one under the other → eye into weird pattern"

📷 趋势 #1: Augmented Reality / Natural Behavior

"What I mean by that is if we can use the camera to cast the lighting and shadows of the real world in a real way onto skeuomorphic elements, that would deserve its own trend."

A. Real-world Lighting / Shadows

Device camera 投射真实环境光

Skuomorphic elements 有真实阴影

Metalmorphism 真正发挥：button 反射你的脸

B. Bendy Buttons（物理反应）

"Buttons that react exactly where you push them"

点 button 边缘 → button 弯向那侧

另一侧有 lighting effect + shadow 变化

✓ Malewicz 已 prototype + 集成到 Longevity Deck

💡 核心技术驱动

"Using the processing power of those modern devices because they are quite powerful, our phones and our computers, to do something a little bit even more realistic than before."

→ 设备性能已够 → 真实物理 UI 终于可行

✏️ 趋势 #2: Hand-drawn / Handwritten

"There is so much AI slop everywhere, we're coming back to a point where we see a button that's just completely hand-drawn with a little bit uneven edge, and the pen was maybe pushed a little bit too hard on one end, so the line is even thicker on some side."

3 大特征

More Human

人性化

More Faulty

不完美 / flawed

More Personal

个人化

例子

  • 漂亮 portfolio 站点（handwritten 风格）
  • 跑步 app 画 run 轨迹（handwritten style）
  • Malewicz 1999 个人网站 = scanned pencil sketches（"actually exploring this trend in the '90s"）

💡 核心驱动

"People might crave these new interactions or these new handwritten or hand-drawn elements... because surrounding us from every direction and every website looking exactly the same."

→ AI 泛滥 → 用户渴望 差异化 + 人性化

Malewicz 给设计师的建议

"Designers can now use these modern tools to prototype things like that. Figure it out. Not every app or website will need skeuomorphism of any kind. But you can try new things, imagine it, build it, see if it works, and then discard it or use it."
"The curious people will push design into some interesting new categories. Maybe even a new morphism."
"As long as we are curious, as long as we want to play around with that stuff, as long as we have those bendy buttons and those realistic shadows and lightning effects, then I think we will be fine as an industry."
