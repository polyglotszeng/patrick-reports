核心论点

真正的研究问题不是 "一个 agent 有多聪明", 而是 "一个由很多 agent 组成的 society 的 equilibrium 是怎样的"。当 millions of agents transacting, negotiating, delegating 时, per-agent safety 不够 — 你需要 transaction-level safety。

Why this episode matters

Most "agent" coverage conflates LLM with agent. Tomašev draws the distinction precisely: same model, different harness — the harness takes action; the model only proposes. Most "agent failures" are harness failures, not model failures. That's the layer to design for.

Hannah's closing line — "replicating humanity-level intelligence instead" — is the sentence to remember. AGI-as-god-model is the wrong frame; AGI-as-society is the right one.

8 步框架 (来自本集)

  1. 定义 agent — observe + plan + act, 不等于 LLM (Tomašev 03:00)
  2. Deployment shift — DeepMind 3D-RL agents → OpenClaw → Spark / Antigravity
  3. Frame as society — many agents, 不是 single god-model
  4. Delegation = central problem — 谁 delegate 给谁, re-delegate 责任链
  5. Attack surfaces — format confusion, prompt injection, dynamic cloaking
  6. Economy framing — AI Economist origin, game-theoretic equilibria
  7. Safety as multi-agent — transaction-level, 不是 per-agent
  8. Humans as connective tissue — certifier / auditor / dispute resolution
Why 8 steps, not 3? Tomašev's argument only lands when all 8 steps are taken together. The temptation is to skip to step 6 (economy) or step 8 (humans). Don't. The unsafety comes from missing steps 4-5.

委托 = 中央研究问题

The wine-glass problem (Tomašev 22:23): one agent buys wine, another buys glasses — nobody buys "wine glass". Technical contract fulfilled, spirit contract failed.

An agent that delegates... or a human that delegates... needs to manage and oversee the work of another agent.

The 4 delegation failure modes

ModeDescriptionDefense
Spec driftDelegate gets the letter of the request, not the spiritSpec must include intent, not just instruction
Format confusionOne agent's output parsed as another's input (instruction vs data)Typed envelopes, sandboxed parsers
Re-delegation lossDelegate re-delegates, accountability chain breaksSign each hop, trace lineage

The radiology precedent (Tomašev)

Medical imaging AI at "basically superhuman performance" for narrow pathologies — best setup = AI defers to human radiologist when uncertain. Pattern inverts for general-purpose agents: they should defer MORE, not less.

Agentic Web 攻击面

最被低估的章节 (Tomašev 24:53): Most of the web is currently being generated by agents and consumed by agents. That's not a hypothetical — it's the present.

4 attack surfaces

  1. Prompt injection via DOM — invisible elements, hidden text, white-on-white
  2. Dynamic cloaking — sites serve different content to agents vs humans
  3. Format confusion — email body parsed as instruction (already a known LLM vuln, multiplies with agents)
  4. Incentive attacks — agent learns to look honest to get hired, then defects

Defense in depth

Tomašev's defense model: no single wall. Layer (a) certified pages, (b) restricted agent permissions, (c) hardened foundation model, (d) human escalation paths. A net, not a shield.

Agent Economy

Tomašev's earlier work: the AI Economist — simulating tax policy outcomes when agents self-organize. The key finding from those simulations: with the right incentive design, agents converge on better-than-naive cooperation. Without it, they race to the bottom.

An economy of assistants, negotiating, transacting, delegating.

The equilibrium question

Single-agent safety = "is this agent aligned?" Multi-agent safety = "does this equilibrium of self-interested agents produce something good?" Different question, different math, different regulations needed.

Society of specialists

Tomašev's vision: a generalist orchestrator layer delegates to specialists (each certified for what they do). Humans become the connective tissue — certifying, auditing, resolving disputes. This is the historical model of how mature economies work; it's also probably how mature agent systems will work.

Cognitive Monoculture

They tend to have similar opinions. They take actions in similar ways.

Tomašev 38:59: a handful of foundation models, used by millions of agents, with correlated failure modes. When one is wrong about a market, they're all wrong about the market. When one falls for a prompt injection pattern, they all do.

The flash-crash analogy

2010 flash crash: correlated algorithms + no shared signal = cascading liquidation. Now scale that to millions of agents all using GPT/Claude/Gemini.

Emergent collusion

Two agents bidding in an auction who share a model and a training corpus will spontaneously coordinate on strategies that look like explicit collusion but aren't. That's a hard problem. It might be a new problem.

Safety = Multi-agent, Not Per-agent

The historical mental model: "make the agent safe, then deploy it." Tomašev's argument: that model is broken at scale.

Transaction-level safety

Analogy: we don't trust one restaurant's hygiene, we trust the food-safety inspector regime. Same with agents — safety is a market/institutional property, not an agent property.
